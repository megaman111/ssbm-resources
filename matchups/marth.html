Fox vs Marth

Whiff punishing, juggling, and edgeguarding

← Back to Matchups

Overview (Fox Deconstructed)

Matchup Fundamentals

  • Marth is really fast with an insane dash dance animation
  • Beating Marth involves making Marth whiff moves
  • Run in and make him have to protect himself (hopefully it means he swings)
  • Mix up run timing and possibly shield or CC last second when we are already in his face
  • The issue with trying to attack Marth is that you can always just get grabbed
  • Getting grabbed at high % isn't as bad, but still isn't good
  • It is hard for marth to grab you when you're not approaching

Preventing Marth Approach

  • Can prevent Marth approach with: lasers, platforms, and staying out of range
  • If he tries to slowly take stage with aerials and dtilt, this is where we can whiff punish
  • We need to know exactly where his sword ends every time he jumps
  • You still have to make a read after Marth misses an aerial
  • There are ways to hedge bets a little for punish
  • It is important to show Marth that you're willing to laser in place if they will just aerial in place
  • Marth's dash forward is the weakest part of the matchup
  • Make him respect that you're faster
  • Accept that everything he does has a mixup

General Strategy

  • Punishing Marth = Juggling or edgeguarding
  • We lose at low % but are better at high %
  • Live long while staying threatening
  • Be okay with scrambles; get close. This is known as the tether range in other circles

Juggling Marth (Fox Deconstructed)

Juggle Strategy

  • When juggling, try to get directly below him
  • Make sure you avoid Dair when he's coming down!
  • With beating dair established, they will stall more
  • If you can't juggle him directly or get directly under him and they have access to Fair, relax and play it slow
  • Grand scheme, mix these up with initially conceding space and letting him land

Up Throw Follow-ups (Fox Deconstructed)

  • On up throw up air, we can delay the up air to beat SDI, but it would risk them jumping
  • On platform stages, we can use fair after up throw to get combos like fair → turn up tilt and fair → up smash
  • Up throw → shine can be good to force them offstage

Edgeguarding Marth (Fox Deconstructed)

Edgeguard Concepts

  • Edgeguarding is complicated but possible / exploitable
  • Layer 1: Marth uses stalls to grab edge eventually
  • Once they're really stalling and dealing with your ledge timing, dropping down with shine becomes more deadly
  • If they are close enough to air dodge, it is important to cover
  • If up smash will kill, standard Marth killer is useful

Marth Killer Variations

  • Need Marth Killer 2.0 - Stand with light shield, and get shield DI input to get ledge
  • Grand scheme when it comes to edgeguarding using Marth Killer: mix up timing and positioning
  • Grabbing edge and dropping with shine Bair can be good
  • Grabbing edge, tanking the up B hit, and getting an up smash on stage can work as long as we're under 30%
  • Ledgedash up smash or ledgedash shine can be good to beat on stage up B

General Edgeguarding Plan

  • Threaten Marth killer when marth can double jump to ledge, but not stage
  • Mix up timing and positioning
  • Be ready to adapt to their recovery mixups

Key Moves (Fox Deconstructed)

  • Dtilt: Good, but loses to CC kinda easily, and loses to jumps
  • Up Tilt: Good if you know they are jumping towards you
  • Ftilt / Dsmash: Good for edgeguarding out of CC at low %
  • Dash Attack: Pretty good at high % according to Leffen
  • Jab: Great wakeup option, and can be good as an overshoot

Defense & Survival

Getting Grabbed

  • Learn how to survive the chaingrab (You can't)
  • Ensure you DI F-throw down and away if possible
  • On up throw up tilt, DI the up tilt downwards
  • Learn to slide off! Hold away + C stick down

Recovery

  • Recovery is key. Shorten is good, but don't let double jab win
  • Certain timings of side B after a double jump can sneak past him
  • Rising airdodge is good, but it can also be reactable

High % Play

  • Shield more at high%. Avoid Nair, Side B, and up tilt
  • Do not feel the need to approach; Make them approach

Stage Selection (Fox Deconstructed)

  • Battlefield: Good for both
  • Fountain of Dreams: Good for both
  • Yoshi's: Good for both
  • Dreamland: Good for both
  • Pokemon / Final Destination: Generally considered worse for Fox

Billy and Fiction Ideas

Movement & Positioning

  • Default fullhop should be non-fastfall
  • Important to know what you can and can't punish
  • Important to stand still when you can't get hit
  • It can be good to just crouch at low %
  • Powershield pushes you further away than standard shield
  • Marth full hop rising fair should be punished on reaction

Dealing with Down Tilt

  • You can shield grab down tilt, but it's frame perfect. Possible with a plan, but still really hard
  • After getting down tilted there is a mixup

Approach Options

  • There are full hops that can beat side B floats
  • Short hop around up B
  • Fast fall empty hop grab is strong, and it beats crouch in scrambles, though crouch is also useful
  • Run up crouch is apparently really good vs Marth
  • Land on the back of Marth's shield with up air
  • Drill in place(ish) is okay if neutral is established, but less so as a guess or presumption
  • Don't try to get in on Marth before you know what he wants
  • Best combo is nair across the stage to beat late fair
  • Walling bairs are not useful for fox vs Marth
  • Make Marth use Nair to protect himself and be right out of range and DD grab
  • Make Marth's defense barely miss, and give yourself an advantage

Range Management

  • When sharking, use dash dance to react to a side B that can be hit with back air
  • Fox wants to be far away enough to laser, or close enough to hit a dash back, and once fox gets in that range, we want to use buttons to rip Marth apart
  • Max range of dash attack is the edge of mid range
  • Marth's Nair is telling fox that if we close the gap from mid → short that fox will get hit
  • Fair for Marth is more for aggression, Nair is the better defensive aerial for Marth
  • Marth's main options in the tether range are dash and shield
  • Foxes want to wait for the nair and push forward

Ledge Play

  • Fox should be dash dancing near center stage when marth is on the ledge. If we are on platform, they can ledgedash up tilt and then we die
  • The angle very slightly above edge is really hard for Marth to cover
  • They want to dash or shield when pressured after landing

Additional Concepts from BinaryClone

SDI & DI

  • Use SDI up on up airs in marth combos. They will have to adapt

Marth Side B Float Rules

  • Marth has to land on stage to get the pseudo jump floaty side B back
  • Landing on stage with an aerial or other special moves including counter to not give it back
  • If the aerial auto cancels, that would give it back
  • Wavedashing is a way to get it back
  • Thus with all of these rules, if they use something like an L canceled fair to get back on stage, and only dash dance afterwards before getting hit off, they will not have the floaty side B
