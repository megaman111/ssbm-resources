Fox vs Fox

The ditto - neutral game, punish, and edgeguarding

← Back to Matchups

Neutral Game

Core Philosophy

  • In neutral we have the choice between high risk and low risk options
  • Judge whether the other fox is looking for big hits, or baits
  • "Attacking in the fox ditto is not that good"
  • We want to get the other fox to overextend into us, and then we punish them for it
  • Goal is to stay on the ground. Fox is fast on the ground
  • The fox who wants an interaction to happen is likely at a disadvantage unless something has happened in neutral to corner the other fox
  • You want neutral to last longer because then you have more chance to outplay
  • Do not commit in positions that feel even

Movement & Positioning

  • When you're not "doing anything" your position still needs to be dynamic
  • Do not play at a certain range; try to move around and look for certain ranges you can take advantage of
  • Need to be 50% disinterested in the opponent. Occasionally just…feel like going to some other position
  • Remember that you can move in a random direction irrelevant to the opponent's position
  • If the battle doesn't look immediately winning, you can do some sort of safe, tricky movement and see if the new position yields better odds
  • Step 1 - Get safe. Step 2 - start moving in tricky ways to find a way in
  • Use movement rather than actual moves to prevent fox moving forward
  • Do not do more than 1 thing in place. You must use his dash to create ambiguity
  • If we can dash dance around basic approaches / distance, we can whiff punish aerials

Drill

  • Fadeback drill is a nice option to hold space with
  • Fadeback drill → Dash forward beats waiting
  • Fadeback drill → Jab or Dash back or Shine can beat whiff punish attempts
  • Drill in place will beat majority of running shine approaches
  • To beat a fadeback drill you need to play the mixup after - are they going to dash forward? dash back? full hop?
  • Answers could be up smash, overshoot running shine, up air/nair, respectively
  • Drill → Shine → Wavedash → Dash → Let dash finish → React
  • Drill grab is good, but do not reach with drill. Drills should be done in place. If you want to reach, reach with late nair
  • While fadeback drill is good for preventing things like running shine, it is not a good approach. Don't do it
  • If you're getting drilled a lot: shield more

Full Hop

  • Full hop is really good in neutral because Fox isn't naturally going to be prepped to beat it like say a Falco, so he'll have to read it if he wants to win the situation
  • Out of full hop you can fadeback drill, waveland back, double jump, shine → waveland on platform
  • Full hop fadeback drill is especially good because it's essentially the same situation as a normal fadeback drill only you have more time to play a mixup out of the full hop
  • It takes some time to set up and for that reason shouldn't be spammed but instead should be used in tandem with a short hop drill/up smash to beat Foxes who like to get real close to punish a landing
  • Fullhop is strong on tri-plat stages because it gives access to platforms
  • Conversely, the lack of having access to all of the above options is why fullhop is significantly worse/riskier on FD
  • Before using fullhop, opponent should be buttered up; very ready to commit
  • Fullhopping over a fox that's coming down is stronger than trying to grab their landing
  • If you do a non-dash fullhop, it's pretty likely you just need to get out to the top platform
  • Shine OOS, dash dance grab, and Fullhop over the other fox are the 3 things that actually work to outplay the opponent in the ditto

Shine

  • If we want to be the faster, more aggressive fox, running shine is clearly good
  • Shine dair is a decent approach, especially if it hits. Can set up for a tech chase
  • If the running shine is likely to miss, shine nair, shine → fullhop, or going into a wavedash may be better than drilling
  • Shinegrab is very good on shield
  • Don't shine any closer than inner platform edge on Battlefield
  • Running shine should be something that happens after extended neutral, more as a last resort
  • If you sense they like to running shine, use up tilt if your back is turned, and drill in place if facing forward
  • Shinegrab into the corner is good, but not great. If the shine hits, they just get edge anyway
  • Wakeup shine dair to beat people waiting to punish waveshine lag
  • If you whiff an aerial, don't shine. Keep moving
  • Use CC shine vs approaching nair / FH bair

Grab

  • Run in grab works once run in shine is established
  • Raw grab is a strong whiff punish; can beat shield, and maybe shine
  • Beat up tilt spam with DD grab / upsmash
  • Nair → grab is really strong at low %
  • If they shine out of shield a lot, it means that they shield a lot. Grab them

Aerials

  • Use fadeback aerials to prevent fox from coming in
  • Retreating Bair is best used against aerial approaches
  • If they are approaching a lot, bair, nair, and up tilt become very useful
  • Nair is a good air to air
  • If you use nair, it should be late nair, especially into the corner
  • Attacks in place make the opponent want to whiff punish

Lasers

  • Laser → Wait. Don't shoot more than 1 laser

Platform Play

  • After double jumping to the top platform, we need to waveland down, rather than wavelanding off automatically. It is too easy to counter
  • Do not attack directly from the top platform. We need drill grab; we're not getting it from up there
  • The only good approach from the top platform is to short hop off the platform, and fastfall through the side platform

Percent-Based Neutral

  • At 0%: We have 3 moves that work: Shine, Dair, and Grab
  • At 5%: Up Air starts to knock down, therefore FH falling up air is on the table
  • At 20%: Dtilt and Up Tilt become useful; they knock down. Up Air 2 on its own also useful near 20%
  • At 35%: Strong Nair and Bair knock down and become useful for keeping the other fox away
  • At 60%: Even weak nair and bair knock down
  • At 100%: Aerials start to kill, Up smash kills, Dash attack → Tech chase up smash becomes good
  • At 100%+ smart foxes will shield even more to beat the dash attack; grab them
  • When you are at low % vs a high % Fox, play even safer

Punish Game

Combo Starters & Extensions

  • Up tilt beats falling drill, and even a trade makes for a great combo
  • Waveshine dtilt is strong
  • Waveshine up smash beats DI up on thunders' combo
  • Wavedash in up tilt is good under these conditions: Dash dance is at stalemate, You can convince them to chase you slightly after dashing away, The space has been maintained and you think they will close the distance
  • Weak reverse bair is a great DI mixup, allowing for combos into up smash or hard bair. At "worst" you will likely get a tech chase
  • Note: Upsmash is very good at low % but gets worse near 20-30%. DI away gets hard to chase

Throw Game

  • If you can always throw fox off stage. Forward and back throw are both good, and largely lead into 50/50s for the edgeguard
  • If you can confirm that they want to DI off a platform to either get to top platform or get off stage, back throw up smash / tech chase should work
  • Up throw → Fast fall → Laser Land / Waveland becomes a kill confirm on side platform

FD Chain Grab (0-66%)

  • 0-40%: You should just up throw
  • If they DI towards the edge/offstage, shine them, or maybe Fthrow them offstage
  • Near 40%: If they use no DI, up tilt
  • Regrab DI away until 66% where Fair / soft Bair knock down → tech chase
  • Probably pivot grab both slight DI. DD grab might work too
  • Only need pivot up smash for no DI. Slight DI can be DD up smashed
  • Above 66%: Upthrow → Fair → RTC until up throw up smash kills

Platform Punishes

  • If up thrown to platform, ensure you mix up your techs
  • On stages with platforms, up throw up tilt is largely bad
  • At low% reading tech in place on platforms with rising up air is good
  • Up air can shield poke on yoshis; it can poke feet on platforms

Reversals & Defense

  • If you can get reversaled, avoid the situation. Being reversal-proof is very useful
  • Ditto is really about edgeguarding, not damage

Edgeguarding

Edgeguard Flowchart

  • Can we get to edge before them? If so take the edge. The fox wants the edge, especially using side B to get there fast. Prevent this if possible
  • If we can't get to the ledge first, can we prevent them from getting to the ledge easily?
  • If we can't prevent them from getting the ledge first, we simply leave
  • Dash attack → grab edge is true. Do not let them side b to edge with no double jump
  • Largely, the goal is to funnel them towards an armada shine

Covering Side-B

  • Edge cancel bair prevents most side B heights
  • Look to use down angle ftilt or dsmash on reaction to side B to the edge
  • After using bair against side B and once it's established and they start to use shorten side B, use late stand
  • During an edgeguard, use the first refresh to cover side B

Additional Edgeguard Tips

  • Never dash attack except at very high % or during the edgeguard
  • If Fox is on the ledge, good positioning lies just outside of BF platform distance

Defense & Recovery

Defensive Options

  • When they are invincible, be very reactive
  • Wavedash OOS → Grab is possible vs up smash on shield
  • If you are in shield, a prime option is to wavedash back to create space, and then go into dash dance
  • If you use shine out of shield to counter someone that is fast fall → L cancel-ing, they will tech, and likely will tech in place

Recovery

  • After sliding off the stage, you can try to immediately side B to edge
  • Up B to ledge from straight above or even over the stage can be useful

Stage-Specific Strategies

Final Destination

  • On FD, value is shifted towards grabs and stage control, given the power of chain grabs and lack of platforms to escape to once cornered
  • Fullhop is significantly worse/riskier on FD due to lack of platform access

Stage Selection

  • Battlefield and Dreamland
  • Yoshi's and Fountain of Dreams
  • FD and Pokemon
