      Fiction

      The 1 and 3 mixup pattern

      This is a basic mixup pattern that Fiction proposes for playing Fox against Sheik in neutral. You do not want to fight Sheik when you can’t do this.
      The 1 and 3 come from counting/numbering how many times we dash dance before we attack. So a “1” is just running in and attacking. A “2” would be dashing away, then dashing in and attacking. A “3” would be dashing in, then dashing away, then dashing in and attacking. And so on. 3 is the most powerful against Sheik - almost everything you do should be a variation on the 3.
      When you do attack and dash in on Sheik, you have 4 options:
      • Running shine
      • Running nair
      • Running grab
      • Fullhop over -> aerial
      There is nothing that Sheik can do to cover all 4 of these options. When you threaten her with one of these 4, she’s gonna get antsy, and she’ll probably do an ftilt or wavedash back - she has to do something to deal with it, but she can’t deal with all of them.
      The big mistake many players make is that they want to dash in, dash out, and then react to what Sheik does. This is the wrong mentality. Always force the Sheik to react to your option. If you play against Sheik and say “I’m gonna bait them” and dash back and watch the Sheik, by the time you see the ftilt it’s too late to punish it - she is borderline impossible to punish on reaction. Sheik’s walling with ftilts and fairs is actually very vulnerable, but you can’t punish it on reaction. But Sheik has to do something to deal with this big mixup Fox has, and if she doesn’t she’s gonna get run over.
      The 3 generally looks like a medium-length dash in (give Sheik something to react to) followed by a short dash back, then a dash in and instant attack. You don’t want to try to react. If Sheik throws out a move, the dash back will give you just enough timing mixup to beat her. Once they catch on a little and start to wait, the 1 starts to work really well.
      Because Sheik can’t cover everything, Fox ends up with a favorable mixup - anything Fox does Sheik can technically punish him for, but Fox has like 10 things that kill Sheik if they work, and she can only kill Fox is she guesses correctly which of those 10 things Fox is going to do.
      When Fiction plays a Sheik he just asks “Will I do a 1 or a 3? I’ll do a 1. What’ll I do after the 1? Running shine.” He doesn’t actually pay attention to the Sheik player. The more you pay attention the more you’ll try to react to what Sheik is doing, and the more you’ll lose. She’s the only character where it’s worse if you try to play neutral against her. You can’t say as Fox, “I’m gonna wait for you to make a mistake,” because that’s what Sheik wants. But Fox wants her to constantly feel like Fox is rolling the dice and throwing out a move - which you should, because that’s the hardest thing for her to deal with.
      Fiction calls dash in fullhop attack a “sneaky” 3 - you move in with the dash in, then you move out of Sheik’s threat range temporarily with the fullhop over her, then you try to hit her. It’s the same concept of Fox threatening and Sheik having to respond, then immediately punishing her after her response.

      Clown Nairs, Not Dairs

      “Clown nairs” are basically just Fox nairs that are kind of just mindless rushdown nairs. It’s hard for Sheik to deal with Fox just jumping in with a nair, and Fox gets to clip her a lot of the time.
      On the flipside, Fox’s short hop dair in neutral is not as good against Sheik, because she’s kind of skinny, and will frequently pop out of the dair and grab Fox even without really good SDI. Drill tends to be better as a whiff punish, because the dair in neutral leads to Fox getting grabbed a lot of the time. Dair in neutral will mostly be limited to the fullhop portion of the mixup, where Fox can react to Sheik’s response and whiff punish her with the drill.

      Updown Lasers

      If Fox wants to start a match with lasers, he should do what Fiction calls updown lasers. This just means drop through the platform with a laser, then fullhop double laser back onto the platform, and repeat. This can be infuriating for Fox’s opponent, and is a good tool for Fox when he’s not feeling very aggro. He doesn’t want to react to Sheik, but can just avoid her entirely using platforms. Typically Fox wants to use these lasers to get Sheik to reach a point where 1 is unreactable, and at that point you have your 1 and 3 mixup pattern.
      The only thing Sheik can do about this is standing needles, which requires a hard read. If Sheik commits to trying to needle Fox on the platform, Fox can just choose to run under her platform and upair her instead of continuing to laser.
